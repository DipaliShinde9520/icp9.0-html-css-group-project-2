Indoor Plants

     Indoor plants are a popular choice for adding greenery and a touch of nature to interior spaces. They come in various shapes, sizes, and care requirements, making them versatile options for different environments.

Outdoor Plants

     A nursery is a place where plants are propagated and grown to a desired size. Mostly the plants concerned are for gardening, forestry, or conservation biology, rather than agriculture, they are essential to gardening and landscaping, offering various options for enhancing indoor and outdoor spaces.

Plant Seeds / Herbs

     Seeds are vital for plant reproduction, allowing plants to spread to new areas and ensuring the continuation of plant species. They are also a crucial part of the human diet, providing grains, legumes, nuts, and other foods rich in essential nutrients. Seeds are used in agriculture, horticulture, and reforestation efforts, making them indispensable in food production and environmental conservation.

     Herbs are versatile plants used for culinary, medicinal, and aromatic purposes. They are typically easy to grow and can be cultivated in gardens, containers, or even indoors on windowsills.

Plants Care

     Houseplants have care requirements that differ from plants grown outdoors. Moisture, light, soil mixture, temperature, ventilation, humidity, fertilizers, and potting are all important factors. Each plant species has different care requirements, and care requirements can vary based on the specific pot, location in a particular home and potting mix used.
